TheArquive Manifesto

02 — Founder's Note

Archivist of Possible Futures

The short version explains what TheArquive is. This note explains why it had to exist.

Open the archive

I do not think I simply have too many projects.

I think I have too many ways of beginning the world.

For years, every idea that crossed me arrived with a body of its own: a name, a folder, an aesthetic, a promise. Sometimes it was a book. Sometimes an app. A website. A manifesto. A game. A tool. A brand. An entire system taking shape before there was even time to ask whether it should exist.

From the outside, perhaps it looks like dispersion. But from within, it was never only chaos.

It was an attempt to preserve possibilities before they disappeared. Not out of vanity, but because some ideas, even unfinished ones, deserve to leave a trace.

The problem was never a lack of imagination. It was a lack of cartography.

TheArquive was born from that. Not as a polished portfolio. Not yet.

Before curation, memory. Before authority, inventory. Before the future, ground.

This is where everything enters: the good, the unfinished, the failed, the excessive, the almost, the absurd, the promising, the dead, and the things that still breathe without moving.

If it existed as an impulse, it deserves a record.

The portfolio comes later. There, the knife enters.

The archive says: this passed through me. The portfolio will say: this is what I can deliver to the world.

In the end, TheArquive is a way of turning excess into system. A place where dispersion stops being a source of shame and becomes organised matter.

Archive Logic

Register everything. Curate the strongest. Execute the possible.

  • If it existed as an impulse, folder, file, domain, text, prototype or attempt, it deserves an entry.
  • Dead projects stay in the record. Failure also reveals direction.
  • The archive does not force every project forward. It prevents disappearance.
